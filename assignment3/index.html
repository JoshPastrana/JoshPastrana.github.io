GUITAR: A stringed instrument played by strumming or plucking its six strings, with fingers pressing frets to change notes and chords.

BASS - The bass guitar has thick strings, played by plucking, providing deep notes. Players press the fretboard to adjust pitch and build rhythm.

VIOLIN - Has four strings played with a bow. Pressing the fingerboard changes pitch, making it ideal for expressive, fast melodies.

SAXAPHONE - A very popular wind instrument. Players blow through a reed mouthpiece and press keys to produce smooth, jazzy tones.

FLUTE - Produces its sound by blowing across its mouthpiece. Covering finger holes changes pitch, creating light and airy tones.

PIANO - This classic instrument creates sound by striking keys, which trigger hammers that hit strings. Its great for solos, accompaniment, and ensembles.

DRUMS - Played by striking toms and symbals with sticks or hands, creating beats that drive rhythm across various musical genres.

HARMONICA - This is played by blowing or drawing air through reeds, producing soulful melodies perfect for blues and folk music.
