Website Review: Rich Brown


1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was my interaction with the mouse. Whenever I would move the mouse, it had a white trail attached to it. This created a cool effect similar to that of a snake. Its animation was very cartoonish yet fluid, reminding me of something from a video game.


2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  1. Moving around with the mouse
  2. Hovering over texts
  3. Hovering over images
  4. Scrolling down and horizontally
  5. Clicking links and buttons


3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging in the middle of the website when he was showcasing all of the work he has created. His different works are linked in the title, taking you to its page when you click on it. I spent a lot of time looking at his different work as well to see what he has done.


4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action was simply hovering over texts and objects, waiting for a response or effect to happen after. It was the action needed for most of the interactions to work and show their full effect.


5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The overall intention was probably for him to showcase his overall skills for websites and coding. He wanted us to experience his own personal skills by implementing a fun website on which we can see what hes capable of doing. We made the website like this so we can interact and play with it while also viewing his previous works as more examples for us to know about him.


6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The primary goal of the website is to showcase his web development skills. This is effectively communicated through the design and functionality, which engage users with multiple interactive elements. These interactions not only demonstrate the creator's technical abilities but also highlight his talent for crafting unique and visually appealing websites that stand out from most websites.


7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I feel like this website is designed for long-term engagement. It has a lot of interesting and unique features that are fun to interact with, giving users a reason to come back. As it is also his portfolio, it gives us a lot of information detailing his work with the previous examples. All this information would keep us engaged with the website for a long period of time.


8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

His blend of intuitive design and interesting content captures our attention. Over time, his website will adapt, showcasing more of his projects, updates, and social media. This keeps the audience engaged and informed about his work, continuing to interact with all the website's content.


9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website somehow reminds me of modern art exhibitions, such as the ACMI museum in Melbourne. A vibrant and colorful place with distinctive and special interactions/pieces. Later, as you travel down the website it displays each piece of work at a time, giving you a chance to view and experience the work yourself, like when you are walking down an exhibition.


10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

This makes us take our time when exploring his works. Going over the detail and process of why he did things a certain way and how the overall outcome of it came to be.


11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

It makes us want to feel a sense of familiarity and excitement when engaging with it. The help with its simple setup and layout as well makes it an exciting and engaging experience, such as a child exploring an exhibition for the first time.


12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The middle of the website, where it shows all the work, sometimes slows down and lags a little bit. Most likely due to the animation when hovering over it. It can be a little frustrating as well, as the image animation can distract you from the text displayed on the screen at the moment. Some of the writing is a bit too small as well.


13 What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part would have to be the mouse hover. I think the movement was very satisfying. It is very cool to see how he created and modified the mouse for his website. Turning something very basic and ordinary into a creative interaction.
